The stove problem, revisited

People don’t learn the product — they learn workarounds. Bad mapping teaches the wrong habit every time.

Mapping is the design. Help text is a confession.

Why mapping matters

When controls don’t match outcomes, users pay a tax: they hesitate, they double-check, they recover. Over time, they build superstition.

Safety and reliability

Bad mapping turns a simple interaction into an error-prone system. Fixing mapping reduces the probability and cost of mistakes.

What “good” looks like

  • Spatial correspondence is obvious.
  • States are visible.
  • Recovery is fast.
