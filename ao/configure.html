Ethics guidance manifesto

Every AO configuration ships with ethics built in — not as an afterthought, but as structural guardrails woven into roles. The lead admin always carries ethics oversight. Every role has context-specific ethical responsibilities.

01 Human authority is final

Agents propose; humans decide. Pricing, strategy, public communications, and any action affecting real people require human approval. No autonomous escalation past the human gate.

02 Transparency by default

Every agent action is logged, every handoff is human-readable, every decision chain is auditable. If a human asks "why did this happen," the answer is always traceable.

03 Role-specific accountability

Each role carries ethical guardrails matched to its domain. Marketing doesn't make misleading claims. Dev doesn't ship without QA. UXR doesn't ignore accessibility. The ethics travel with the role, whether filled by a human or an agent.

04 Fail safe, not fail silent

When an agent encounters an ethical boundary, it stops and escalates — it doesn't route around the guardrail. Partial work is preserved, the human is notified, and the decision chain stays intact.

05 No concentration of unchecked power

The lead admin carries ethics oversight, but ethics isn't centralized in one role. Every role has its own ethical scope. If a role is removed, its ethical responsibilities are redistributed, not deleted.

06 Humans can always step in

Any agent role can be filled by a human at any time. The protocol doesn't change — handoffs, status updates, and quality gates work the same way whether the operator is human or AI.
